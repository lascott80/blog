It's finally happening. After more than 30 years without a major update, Walt Disney's Carousel of Progress -- the most performed stage show in the history of American theater -- is getting the refresh it deserves. And honestly? It's about time.

## Why This Update Is Long Overdue

Let's be real: the last major update to the Carousel of Progress was back in 1994. That's before the internet became a household staple, before smartphones, before social media, and certainly before AI-powered everything. For an attraction that's literally about celebrating technological progress, standing still for three decades is kind of ironic, isn't it?

The Carousel of Progress was always designed to evolve. Walt Disney himself never intended it to be a museum piece -- he built it as a living demonstration of how technology transforms everyday life.

When the current finale scene shows a "futuristic" household with a CD burner and a voice-activated oven that pales in comparison to any modern smart speaker, you know it's time for a glow-up.

## What's Changing

For the first time ever, guests will see Walt Disney himself in the attraction, brought to life through Audio-Animatronics technology. He'll appear in a scene inspired by the 1964 TV special *Disneyland Goes to the World's Fair* -- the very moment he introduced the Carousel of Progress to the world. Imagineers are painstakingly recreating props from that special, including the prototype Tiki bird and the Tower of the Four Winds model.

Beyond Walt's debut, every scene is being updated to new time periods that today's audiences actually have personal connections to:

- The 1960s. The family gathers around the television to witness the historic moon landing.
- The 1980s. Halloween night, 1985. Bigger is better, and Sarah takes center stage for the first time ever, showing off new gadgets that make life easier.
- The 1990s. New Year's Eve 1999. The internet arrives, connecting the family and the world like never before.
- The distant future. A space-age home complete with a helpful robot assistant, space travel, and a vision inspired by Disney Legend John Hench's original concept sketches.

And yes, the Sherman Brothers' iconic anthem "There's a Great Big Beautiful Tomorrow" is staying right where it belongs.

## Why This Matters for Both Generations

For longtime Disney fans: this is the update we've been waiting for. You know every line of dialogue by heart. You've sat through the show dozens of times. This refresh doesn't erase what you love -- it builds on it.

For new audiences: if you've never experienced the Carousel of Progress, you're in for something special. This isn't a thrill ride. It's a piece of Walt Disney's soul -- his unwavering belief that tomorrow can be better than today.

There's something uniquely Disney about sitting in a rotating theater, watching an American family navigate progress across the decades, and realizing that through all the change, people really do stay the same.

## When Can We See It?

The Carousel of Progress will close for its transformation on July 6, 2026, with an expected reopening in 2027. So if you want one last ride through the current version, now's your chance.

Personally, I can't wait to see what Imagineers have been cooking up. Walt would be proud.
